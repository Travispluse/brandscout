## Why You Need a Launch Checklist

Most brand launches are chaotic. Founders rush to get the website live and forget critical steps â€” unsecured social handles, missing legal registrations, broken email addresses. A checklist turns chaos into confidence.

## Pre-Launch: 4-6 Weeks Before

### Brand Foundation
- [ ] Brand name finalized and validated
- [ ] Domain name purchased (primary + key alternatives)
- [ ] Social media handles secured on all relevant platforms
- [ ] Trademark search completed (consider filing application)
- [ ] Brand positioning statement documented
- [ ] Brand voice and tone guide written

### Visual Identity
- [ ] Logo designed in multiple formats (full color, black/white, icon-only)
- [ ] Color palette defined with exact hex codes, RGB, and CMYK values
- [ ] Typography selected (primary and secondary fonts)
- [ ] Brand guidelines document created
- [ ] Business card design completed
- [ ] Email signature template created

### Legal and Business
- [ ] Business entity registered (LLC, Corp, etc.)
- [ ] EIN obtained (in the US)
- [ ] Business bank account opened
- [ ] Business insurance arranged
- [ ] Terms of service and privacy policy drafted
- [ ] Any required licenses or permits obtained

## Pre-Launch: 2-4 Weeks Before

### Digital Presence
- [ ] Website built and tested across devices and browsers
- [ ] SSL certificate installed (HTTPS)
- [ ] Email addresses set up (hello@, support@, info@)
- [ ] Google Analytics or preferred analytics installed
- [ ] Google Search Console set up and verified
- [ ] Favicon uploaded
- [ ] 404 page created
- [ ] Meta titles and descriptions written for all pages

### Content
- [ ] Homepage copy finalized
- [ ] About page written with brand story
- [ ] Product/service pages completed
- [ ] FAQ section created
- [ ] Blog launched with 3-5 initial posts
- [ ] Lead magnet or signup incentive created
- [ ] Email welcome sequence written

### Social Media
- [ ] Profile photos and cover images uploaded
- [ ] Bio and descriptions written consistently across platforms
- [ ] Link in bio set up (Linktree or direct)
- [ ] First 5-10 posts scheduled
- [ ] Content calendar created for first month

## Pre-Launch: 1 Week Before

### Technical Testing
- [ ] All website links tested (no 404s)
- [ ] Forms tested (contact, signup, checkout)
- [ ] Email deliverability tested
- [ ] Mobile experience reviewed
- [ ] Page load speed checked (aim for under 3 seconds)
- [ ] Payment processing tested (if applicable)

### Launch Preparation
- [ ] Launch announcement written (email, social, PR)
- [ ] Press kit assembled (logo files, founder photos, brand story, key facts)
- [ ] Friends, family, and network notified of launch date
- [ ] Launch day social media posts scheduled
- [ ] Google Business Profile created (if local business)

## Launch Day

### Execute
- [ ] Publish website (remove any "coming soon" pages)
- [ ] Send launch announcement email
- [ ] Publish social media launch posts
- [ ] Send press release (if applicable)
- [ ] Share in relevant communities (Reddit, Product Hunt, industry forums)
- [ ] Monitor all channels for comments and questions
- [ ] Respond to every piece of engagement within the first 24 hours

### Monitor
- [ ] Watch analytics for any issues
- [ ] Test the full customer journey one more time
- [ ] Check for broken elements or errors
- [ ] Monitor brand mentions using Google Alerts

## Post-Launch: First Week

- [ ] Follow up with press contacts
- [ ] Gather and respond to initial feedback
- [ ] Fix any issues discovered by real users
- [ ] Publish follow-up content (behind-the-scenes, thank you posts)
- [ ] Begin outreach for reviews and testimonials
- [ ] Set up ongoing analytics reporting

## Post-Launch: First Month

- [ ] Review analytics and identify top-performing content
- [ ] Adjust social media strategy based on engagement data
- [ ] Start SEO optimization based on initial search data
- [ ] Develop second month content calendar
- [ ] Begin building email list actively
- [ ] Evaluate which marketing channels deserve more investment

## The Foundation of Everything

This entire checklist assumes you have the right brand name. A name that's available as a domain, claimable across social platforms, and clear of trademark conflicts. If you're not sure about your name yet, that's step zero.
