## Why Your Registrar Choice Matters

A domain registrar is where you buy and manage your brand's web address. While most registrars perform the same basic function, they differ significantly in pricing, renewal costs, included features, and ease of use. Choosing the wrong registrar can cost you hundreds of dollars over time.

## What to Look For

### Transparent Pricing
Some registrars lure you with $0.99 first-year pricing, then charge $20+ for renewals. Always check the renewal price before buying.

### WHOIS Privacy
Your personal information (name, address, phone) is publicly attached to your domain by default. WHOIS privacy protection hides this. Some registrars include it free; others charge $10-15/year.

### DNS Management
You need reliable DNS to point your domain to your website, email service, and other tools. Good registrars offer fast, reliable DNS management with an intuitive interface.

### Security Features
Look for two-factor authentication, domain lock (prevents unauthorized transfers), and DNSSEC support.

### Transfer Policies
You should be able to transfer your domain to another registrar easily. Avoid registrars that make transfers difficult or charge transfer fees.

## Top Registrars Compared

### Cloudflare Registrar
**Best for:** Cost-conscious buyers who want no-markup pricing.

- **Pricing:** At-cost (wholesale ICANN price, no markup). Typically $9-10/year for .com
- **WHOIS Privacy:** Free
- **Renewal Price:** Same as registration (no price jumps)
- **DNS:** Industry-leading DNS performance (Cloudflare is primarily a DNS/CDN company)
- **Notable:** No markup policy means they're consistently the cheapest option. Also includes DDoS protection and CDN services.
- **Drawback:** Interface is more technical. No domain marketplace for premium domains.

### Namecheap
**Best for:** Small businesses wanting good value and easy management.

- **Pricing:** $8-12/year for .com (frequently runs promotions)
- **WHOIS Privacy:** Free for life
- **Renewal Price:** Competitive, clearly displayed before purchase
- **DNS:** Solid free DNS with easy management
- **Notable:** User-friendly interface, good customer support, includes email forwarding.
- **Drawback:** Occasional aggressive upselling during checkout.

### Google Domains (now Squarespace Domains)
**Best for:** Users who want simplicity and Google integration.

- **Pricing:** $12-14/year for .com
- **WHOIS Privacy:** Free
- **Renewal Price:** Same as registration
- **DNS:** Google Cloud DNS (reliable and fast)
- **Notable:** Clean interface, easy Google Workspace integration, email forwarding included.
- **Drawback:** Transferred to Squarespace in 2023 — some users report declining experience.

### Porkbun
**Best for:** Great pricing with a straightforward experience.

- **Pricing:** $8-10/year for .com
- **WHOIS Privacy:** Free
- **Renewal Price:** Transparent, competitive
- **DNS:** Free DNS hosting
- **Notable:** Consistently good pricing, free SSL certificates, email forwarding included. Quirky branding but professional service.
- **Drawback:** Smaller company — some users want the safety of a larger registrar.

### GoDaddy
**Best for:** Non-technical users who want phone support.

- **Pricing:** $12-20/year for .com (promotional pricing misleading)
- **WHOIS Privacy:** $10-15/year additional
- **Renewal Price:** Significantly higher than registration price
- **DNS:** Basic, functional
- **Notable:** Largest registrar by volume. 24/7 phone support. Extensive domain marketplace.
- **Drawback:** Aggressive upselling, hidden costs, renewal price jumps. Most experienced users recommend alternatives.

### Hover
**Best for:** Clean experience with no upselling.

- **Pricing:** $13-15/year for .com
- **WHOIS Privacy:** Free
- **Renewal Price:** Transparent
- **DNS:** Clean management interface
- **Notable:** No-upsell policy. Clean, focused experience. Good customer support.
- **Drawback:** Slightly higher prices than budget options. Fewer advanced features.

## Domain Buying Tips

### Buy for Multiple Years
If you're committed to the name, register for 2-5 years. Some registrars offer multi-year discounts, and it eliminates the risk of forgetting to renew.

### Enable Auto-Renewal
Expired domains can be snatched by domain squatters within hours. Always enable auto-renewal and keep your payment method current.

### Buy Protective Domains
Register common misspellings and alternative TLDs (.co, .net) to redirect to your primary domain. This prevents competitors and squatters from capitalizing on typos.

### Don't Buy Through Your Web Host
Hosting companies often bundle domain registration. This makes it harder to transfer your domain if you switch hosts. Keep domains and hosting separate.
