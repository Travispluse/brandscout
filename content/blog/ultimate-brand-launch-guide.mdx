# The Ultimate Brand Launch Guide: From Name to Launch Day

You've chosen your brand name. Now what? Launching a brand involves dozens of steps between naming and going public. This comprehensive guide walks you through every phase, ensuring nothing falls through the cracks.

## Phase 1: Secure Your Digital Assets (Weeks 1-2)

### Domain Registration
- Register your primary domain (.com if possible)
- Register defensive domains (common misspellings, alternative extensions)
- Set up auto-renewal on all domains
- Enable WHOIS privacy protection
- Configure DNS with a reliable provider

### Social Media Handles
- Claim your name on all major platforms, even ones you won't use immediately:
  - Twitter/X, Instagram, Facebook, LinkedIn, TikTok
  - YouTube, GitHub, Pinterest, Reddit
  - Threads, Bluesky, Mastodon
- Use consistent naming across all platforms
- Upload a placeholder profile image and bio on each

### Email Setup
- Configure business email (name@yourbrand.com)
- Set up SPF, DKIM, and DMARC records for deliverability
- Create key email addresses: hello@, support@, info@

### Other Digital Assets
- App store developer accounts (Apple, Google Play)
- Google Business Profile (if applicable)
- Trademark application (consult an attorney)

## Phase 2: Build Your Brand Identity (Weeks 2-4)

### Visual Identity
- Logo design (primary, secondary, favicon versions)
- Color palette (primary, secondary, accent colors with hex codes)
- Typography (heading and body fonts)
- Photography/illustration style guidelines
- Brand guidelines document

### Brand Voice
- Define your tone: formal or casual? Playful or serious?
- Create a brand voice document with do/don't examples
- Write template copy for common scenarios

### Key Brand Assets
- Business cards
- Email signatures
- Social media profile images and banners
- Letterhead and documents
- Presentation templates

## Phase 3: Build Your Online Presence (Weeks 3-6)

### Website
- Design and develop your website
- Essential pages: Home, About, Product/Service, Contact
- SEO fundamentals: meta titles, descriptions, schema markup
- SSL certificate and HTTPS
- Mobile responsiveness testing
- Page speed optimization
- Analytics setup (Google Analytics, etc.)

### Content Foundation
- Write 5-10 blog posts to launch with SEO momentum
- Create your brand story page
- Develop FAQ content
- Prepare case studies or portfolio pieces (if applicable)

### Social Media Content
- Create a content calendar for the first 30 days
- Prepare 10-20 posts in advance
- Design templates for recurring content types

## Phase 4: Pre-Launch Preparation (Weeks 5-7)

### Build Anticipation
- Create a "coming soon" landing page with email capture
- Start posting teaser content on social media
- Reach out to your network for early support
- Prepare a press kit (logo files, founder bios, brand story, high-res images)

### Email List Building
- Set up email marketing (ConvertKit, Mailchimp, etc.)
- Create a welcome sequence for new subscribers
- Build your initial list through personal network and landing page

### PR and Outreach
- Identify relevant media contacts, bloggers, and influencers
- Write press releases or media pitches
- Prepare founder interview talking points

### Technical Checks
- Test website across browsers and devices
- Verify all links work
- Test email deliverability
- Check analytics tracking
- Review site speed and performance
- Test forms, signups, and payment flows

## Phase 5: Launch Day

### The Launch Sequence
1. **Morning**: Publish website, send announcement email to your list
2. **Mid-morning**: Post across all social media platforms
3. **Afternoon**: Send press releases and personal outreach to media contacts
4. **Evening**: Engage with all comments, shares, and mentions
5. **Monitor**: Watch analytics, fix issues in real-time

### Launch Amplification
- Ask friends, family, and supporters to share your launch posts
- Post in relevant online communities (with genuine value, not spam)
- Consider a Product Hunt launch if you're B2B/tech
- Run targeted social media ads to boost launch content
- Host a launch event (virtual or in-person)

## Phase 6: Post-Launch (Weeks 8+)

### First 30 Days
- Maintain consistent social media posting
- Respond to every comment and message
- Gather early customer feedback
- Monitor brand mentions and reviews
- Analyze website traffic and user behavior
- Adjust strategy based on data

### Ongoing Brand Building
- Publish regular content (blog, social, email)
- Build and nurture relationships with your audience
- Seek partnerships and collaborations
- Monitor competitors
- Iterate on your product/service based on feedback
